## Executive Summary

ClickBank in 2026 is best described as a **stable, cash-generating, niche-dominant platform that is no longer the center of gravity for global affiliate marketing**, but remains the single most accessible network on the planet for an outsider who wants to promote nutra/info-product VSLs on a CPA model. It is BBB A+ accredited, owned by the same private holding (Keynetics) it has always been owned by, paid out USD 7.1B+ in lifetime affiliate commissions, processes ~USD 850M annual revenue, and has a marketplace of ~28,000 listings across 281 categories with Health & Dietary Supplements decisively #1. The Advantage tier program (Bronze→Diamond, launched Jan 2022) is intact, fees are unchanged (7.5% + $1, $49.95 setup), and the company is actively pushing AI tooling (Genius.AI/Ember partnership, Sept 2024) and a server-to-server Meta CAPI integration shipped Jan 2025.

For a **LATAM-based new affiliate (Chile)**, the bet is more nuanced. ClickBank is not banned in Chile, but Chile is **not on the XACH direct deposit list** (43 countries, all of LATAM excluded except Mexico) — payment requires Wise USD ACH details, Payoneer, or wire transfer. The US-Chile tax treaty entered into force Dec 2023 (effective Feb 2024) so a properly filed W-8BEN reduces withholding. The Customer Distribution Requirement (5 sales / 2 payment methods / 2-week wait) and the dormant-account fee schedule ($1/$5/$50 per pay period at 90/180/365 days) are both unchanged and will eat new affiliates who don't get sales fast. The Spanish-language offer ecosystem is real but thin — a handful of "El ProDent"-style translated nutra offers exist, conversion rates are documented as lower than US traffic, and there is no published count or marketplace filter for Spanish offers specifically. **Bottom line: ClickBank is a defensible, lower-risk place for a LATAM beginner to learn paid-traffic nutra; the upside ceiling for Spanish-market scale is real but unproven and the platform is clearly not the future of global affiliate — it's the present of US/Spanish-language nutra info-product VSLs.**

## 4. ClickBank for Non-US Affiliates (LATAM / Chile-Specific)

### Direct deposit (XACH) supported countries — full official list
**[VERIFIED]** — [ClickBank Support, last updated Oct 21, 2025](https://support.clickbank.com/en/articles/10535128-what-countries-does-clickbank-offer-direct-deposit-to):

Australia, Austria, Belgium, Bulgaria, Canada, Czech Republic, Denmark, Finland, France, French Guiana, Germany, Gibraltar, Greece, Guadeloupe, Hong Kong, Hungary, Iceland, India, Ireland, Italy, Liechtenstein, Lithuania, Luxembourg, Martinique, Mayotte, **Mexico** (only LATAM country), Monaco, Netherlands, New Zealand, Norway, Philippines, Poland, Portugal, Reunion Island, Romania, Singapore, Slovak Republic, Slovenia, Spain, Saint Pierre & Miquelon, Sweden, Switzerland, United Kingdom.

**= 43 countries. Chile is NOT on the list. Argentina, Colombia, Peru, Uruguay, Brazil — none on the list either.**

### Payment options for Chilean affiliates
1. **Wise USD ACH workaround — STILL WORKS in 2025.** Open a Wise (formerly TransferWise) Business account; Wise issues you US bank account details (routing + account number); ClickBank treats it as US ACH; you receive USD into your Wise account; convert to CLP via Wise's mid-market rate. Wise integration is officially documented in their AU blog post for ClickBank affiliates. **[VERIFIED]** — [Wise/ClickBank guide](https://wise.com/au/blog/receive-payment-clickbank-wise).
   - Wise transfer fees: ~$4.14 USD per wire if you do wire instead. ACH is free.
   - ClickBank fee: $2.50–$5 per pay period (varies by source).
2. **Payoneer** — official partner. ClickBank → Payoneer is a documented integration. Withdraw to Chilean bank account in CLP from Payoneer. **[VERIFIED]** — [ClickBank Payoneer integration](https://www.clickbank.com/partners/payoneer/).
3. **Wire transfer** — to your Chilean bank in USD. Slow, fees ~$30–50, requires bank support.
4. **Paper check** — slow (3–6 weeks to LATAM), not recommended.

### W-8BEN-E and US-Chile tax treaty
- **US-Chile income tax treaty** entered into force **December 19, 2023**. Withholding effects took hold for amounts paid on or after **February 1, 2024**. **[VERIFIED]** — [US Treasury announcement](https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/jy2003), [Deloitte tax newsletter](https://dhub.deloitte.com/Newsletters/Tax/2024/TNV/240112_3.pdf).
- **Implication for affiliates:** A properly executed W-8BEN (individual) or W-8BEN-E (entity) lets you claim treaty benefits and reduce or eliminate US withholding on royalty/service income.
- **ClickBank's W-form process:** Self-service, automated. When you register, you get a personalized link to complete the W-8BEN online. **[VERIFIED]**
- **Practical note:** US affiliates earning over $600 need a TIN (SSN or EIN). For Chilean affiliates, your Chilean RUT is fine for W-8BEN as foreign TIN; you do not need to obtain a US ITIN/EIN.

### Customer Distribution Requirement (CDR) — still active
- **Requirement: 5 unique sales from at least 2 different payment methods, AND 2 weeks elapsed since the 5th sale.** **[VERIFIED]** — [ClickBank Support CDR page](https://support.clickbank.com/en/articles/10535126-what-is-the-customer-distribution-requirement).
- This is anti-fraud (prevents self-purchase rebate gaming).
- **Important:** Dormant fees are charged even if you haven't yet met CDR. So if you don't get sales, you bleed money sitting in escrow.

### Dormant account fee — current schedule
- **90 days no earnings:** $1 per pay period
- **180 days:** $5 per pay period
- **365 days:** **$50 per pay period**
- Charges stop when account earns again or balance hits zero.
- ClickBank does NOT notify you when account becomes dormant. **[VERIFIED]** — [ClickBank dormant fee page](https://support.clickbank.com/en/articles/10535108-what-happens-if-i-don-t-use-my-clickbank-account-for-a-long-time).
- Mitigation: Lower your payment threshold to drain the account if you'll be inactive.

### Common LATAM rejection patterns
- ClickBank's fraud system silently rejects accounts with no specific reason given.
- **Typical rejection triggers** (from community signals):
  - VPN-based signup
  - Country mismatch (account country vs. IP)
  - Phone number mismatch
  - Reusing a previously banned email/IP
- **If rejected:** Email payments@clickbank.com with country + government photo ID. Often resolves. **[VERIFIED]**

## 6. Competition Assessment 2026

### vs. Amazon Associates
- Different game entirely. Amazon is **46% of global affiliate market share** but commission rates are 1–10%, not 50–75%. Not a substitute for ClickBank's CPA-nutra play. **[INDUSTRY ESTIMATE]** — [OptinMonster affiliate stats 2026](https://optinmonster.com/affiliate-marketing-statistics/).

### vs. MaxBounty
- **MaxBounty edge:** CPL (cost-per-lead) offers, not just CPS. Dedicated affiliate managers. Stronger in straight-line CPA verticals.
- **MaxBounty drawback:** $100 minimum payout (vs. ClickBank's $10). Stricter affiliate approval — they reject many newcomers.
- **For nutra:** MaxBounty has a strong nutra catalog but LATAM access is harder than ClickBank's. Cris/Ro would likely be approved on ClickBank but face friction on MaxBounty.
- **Verdict:** ClickBank wins for accessibility + low entry. MaxBounty wins if you can get approved and want CPL volume.

### vs. Digistore24
- **Digistore24 = the European ClickBank.** Founded 2012. ~8,500 vetted offers across 44 niches (vs. ClickBank's ~28K listings, ~1,247 unique). **[INDUSTRY ESTIMATE]** — [Digistore24 vs ClickBank](https://digistore24.com/en/blog/digistore24-vs-clickbank/).
- **Digistore24 advantages:** Fully EU-compliant (registered EU entity, full VAT handling). Cookie duration up to 180 days (ClickBank: 60). Pays up to 3x/week, no minimum threshold. Supports PayPal, Apple Pay, Google Pay, Klarna.
- **Digistore24 commission range:** 50–85%, sometimes 100% on trials.
- **For Spanish-language affiliates:** Digistore24's localization is German-centric; their Spanish presence is also growing but not yet dominant. **[INDUSTRY ESTIMATE]**
- **Verdict:** **Digistore24 is the most credible threat to ClickBank's nutra dominance, but ClickBank still wins for US-targeting and for the largest pool of established nutra brands.** If your traffic is European, Digistore24 is increasingly the better choice.

### vs. ShareASale (Awin)
- Different vertical — Awin/ShareASale dominate **physical retail and brand affiliate programs**. 30,000+ brands, 225,000 affiliates. Not a substitute for ClickBank's info-product/nutra niche.

### vs. Impact, Rakuten, CJ Affiliate
- **Premium brand networks.** Direct-to-brand programs (not marketplace). Commissions 1–15%, not 50–75%. They're winning **brand affiliate share**, not eating ClickBank's nutra-VSL lunch.

### vs. WarriorPlus / JVZoo
- **Both focused on internet marketing / make-money-online niches** — narrower than ClickBank.
- **JVZoo:** Instant payments, beginner-friendly, 50%+ commissions standard.
- **WarriorPlus:** 14-day waiting period, more advanced/IM-specialist audience.
- **For Spanish-market nutra:** Neither is a meaningful alternative. They're complementary platforms for an MMO-info-product strategy.

### Newer entrants (AI-native networks)
- No credible AI-native affiliate network has emerged at scale in 2024–2026. Some YC-funded creator-economy plays exist but none with ClickBank's nutra inventory.
- **Genius.AI / Ember** is essentially ClickBank's defensive AI-native push from inside (Sept 2024 partnership).

### Bottom line on competition
- **ClickBank still dominates:** US-targeting nutra/info-product VSLs with CPA payouts on a marketplace model.
- **ClickBank is losing share in:** EU-targeting (Digistore24), brand affiliate programs (Awin/Impact/CJ), pure CPL plays (MaxBounty).
- **No platform is "killing ClickBank"** — the affiliate ecosystem is fragmenting by vertical, not consolidating. ClickBank's lane is intact.

## 11. RED FLAGS for a 2026 Newcomer

### Honest red flags
1. **Marketplace size is smaller than the marketing copy suggests.** 28,083 listings sounds big; only 1,247 unique products. Most affiliates are competing on the **same 100 hot offers**.
2. **Customer service complaints (buyer-side)** create churn-back risk. Your conversions are subject to your customer's service experience with ClickBank, not just the seller.
3. **CDR + dormant fee combo punishes slow starters.** A new LATAM affiliate who tests Meta nutra for 6 months without hitting CDR will lose ~$30 to dormant fees plus have funds locked.
4. **Spanish-language inventory is thin** (~20–60 actively-promoted offers) — low ceiling for a Spanish-market thesis.
5. **The "long tail of affiliates" is in structural decline.** ClickBank's own podcast says so. The platform is bifurcating into "super-affiliates with seller relationships" and "noise." Bronze is a hard place to live.
6. **Refund/clawback risk on CPA** can swing a profitable week into a loss if a single bulk-refunded buyer cohort hits.
7. **FTC subscription enforcement risk** — many ClickBank offers are auto-rebill subscriptions; FTC's 2025 enforcement spotlight is exactly here. If a vendor gets hit, your traffic source / your account / your spend can all blow up.
8. **No public Spanish marketplace filter or count** — you're working blind on inventory size and growth.
9. **Ember (the AI tool) is "unavailable for purchase"** — suggests ClickBank's flagship 2024 AI bet is wobbling.
10. **Network effects favor English market.** All Spark education, all Affiliated podcast episodes, all top offers analysis are English-first. As a Spanish-market affiliate, you're consuming education that is one-step-removed from your actual market.

### What ClickBank is NOT
- **Not** a sinking ship.
- **Not** experiencing mass affiliate exodus to MaxBounty/Digistore24/Impact.
- **Not** under FTC enforcement action against the platform itself.
- **Not** facing a credible AI-native disruptor at scale.

## 13. Verdict: Should a New LATAM-Based Affiliate Bet $2,500 USD on ClickBank in 2026?

### Short answer
**Yes — with discipline. ClickBank is the right training-wheel platform for a LATAM affiliate testing the Spanish-nutra-VSL thesis, but the $2,500 should buy education + tracking + first paid-traffic tests, not a "scale fast" play.**

### The reasoning
- **The platform is solid.** No solvency risk, no ban risk for Chile, no FTC risk against the platform itself, payment infrastructure to Chile is solved.
- **The opportunity is narrow but real.** Spanish-language nutra has measurable inventory (El ProDent confirmed, ~20–60 offers community-estimated), proven AOVs ($120–$175), proven creative templates (translated US winners), and lower competition than English market.
- **The risks are manageable** if you respect the CDR + dormant fee dynamics. Translation: get to 5 sales fast or pause the account.
- **The ceiling is uncertain.** No published Spanish-market data, no Spanish-affiliate breakthrough star to model after. Pancho Val is the closest reference but his playbook is largely English-market media buying.

### How I'd structure the $2,500 if I were doing this
- **$200–400** to ClickBank setup ($49.95 vendor activation only if you'll also be a seller; $0 to be only an affiliate) + tracking tools (e.g., Voluum trial, BeMob, RedTrack — many have free tiers).
- **$0** to ClickBank fees (no monthly fee, no affiliate-side activation cost — confirmed).
- **$1,500–1,800** to first paid-traffic tests on Meta and YouTube targeting Spanish nutra. Plan for 4–6 micro-tests at $250–400 each, not one big bet.
- **$200–300** held back for unexpected — currency conversion losses, refund clawbacks, dormant-period buffer.

### What would change my answer to "no"
- If Chile became a banned country (no current signal it will).
- If Wise → ClickBank ACH path broke (no current signal).
- If ClickBank moved to gate marketplace access by income tier (no signal).
- If a sweeping FTC action took out 5+ top nutra vendors (possible but not currently underway).

### What would make me say "go bigger than $2,500"
- A specific Spanish-market vendor relationship secured (e.g., El ProDent publisher willing to give CPA bumps for exclusive LATAM traffic).
- 3 successful $250-test campaigns with positive ROAS.
- Confirmation that a Spanish-market super-affiliate is willing to coach (Pancho Val community, etc.).

### The thing I'd watch most carefully
- **Subscription/rebill FTC enforcement in 2026.** If FTC moves against subscription nutra in a sweeping way, the entire ClickBank nutra ecosystem (which is heavily auto-rebill) could compress overnight. This is the single asymmetric tail risk for the bet.
